## ðŸ“š Grammar Review

### 1. Prepositions of Transportation

**BY** - Used with most forms of transportation:
- by car / by bus / by train / by plane / by taxi / by boat
- Example: "I go to work **by bus**."

**ON** - Used when you can walk inside the vehicle:
- on the bus / on the train / on the plane / on the boat
- Also: on a bike / on a motorcycle / on foot
- Example: "She is **on the train** right now."

**IN** - Used with enclosed private vehicles:
- in the car / in the taxi
- Example: "We are **in the car**."

---

### 2. Present Continuous for Travel
**Form:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

**Use:** For actions happening now or temporary situations

**Examples:**
- I **am traveling** to Rio this week.
- She **is flying** to London tomorrow.
- They **are waiting** at the airport.
- We **are taking** the bus.

---

### 3. "Going to" for Future Plans
**Form:** Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

**Use:** For plans and intentions

**Examples:**
- I **am going to travel** to Paris next month.
- She **is going to visit** her family next week.
- We **are going to fly** to New York.
- They **are going to take** the train.
