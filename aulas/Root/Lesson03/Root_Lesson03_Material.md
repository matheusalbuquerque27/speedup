## üìö Grammar - Future with "Going to"

### Structure:
**Affirmative:** Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb
**Negative:** Subject + am/is/are + not + going to + base verb
**Question:** Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + base verb?

### Use:
For **plans and intentions** you've already decided

### Examples:

**Affirmative:**
- I **am going to have** a party next Saturday.
- She **is going to invite** all her friends.
- We **are going to celebrate** his birthday.
- They **are going to organize** a barbecue.
- You **are going to love** it!

**Negative:**
- I**'m not going to go** to the party.
- She**'s not going to come**.
- We**'re not going to attend** the meeting.
- They**'re not going to join** us.

**Questions:**
- **What are you going to do** this weekend?
- **Where are you going to go** tonight?
- **Who are you going to invite**?
- **When are you going to have** the party?
- **Are you going to come** to my birthday?

**Short Answers:**
- Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
- Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.
- Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.
